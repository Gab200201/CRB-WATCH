Our Battle Plan

Our multi-faceted approach combines science, community, and innovation to stop the spread of the Coconut Rhinoceros Beetle.

Surveillance & Mapping

Using community reports, drones, and GIS to map infestations and target hotspots for efficient management.

Natural & Physical Control

Applying eco-friendly methods like hot water, salt water, and special non-toxic mixtures to disrupt the beetle exoskeleton and kill larvae.

Trapping & Lures

Implementing solar-powered UV light traps, mating sound replication, and netting over compost piles to disrupt breeding cycles.

Community Involvement

Mobilizing society through public education, bounty programs, and engaging local farmers and schools to create a collaborative effort.

The Threat to Our Plants

The Coconut Rhinoceros Beetle is a major pest that damages a wide range of economically and culturally significant plants beyond just palms.

Coconut Palms

This is the primary target. Adult beetles bore into the crown of the palm to feed on sap. This boring action cuts through the developing, folded fronds. When these fronds grow out and unfold, the damage appears as characteristic **V-shaped cuts**. According to studies by the University of Hawaii, severe infestations reduce coconut production and can kill the tree by damaging its single growing point (meristem).

Sugarcane

While a secondary host, the CRB still poses a threat. Adult beetles bore into the soft, upper sections of the sugarcane stalk to feed. This damage creates entry points for fungal and bacterial diseases, such as red rot, which can devastate the crop. The boring weakens the stalk, making it prone to breaking and reducing the overall sugar yield.

Breadfruit ('Ulu)

The beetle's impact on breadfruit is significant as its larvae can thrive in rotting breadfruit and other composted materials, turning cultural practices like using earth ovens into potential breeding grounds. Adults may bore into the soft stems and developing fruit, causing damage that reduces yield and makes the fruit unmarketable.

Taro (Kalo)

CRB is often mistaken for the similar-looking Taro Beetle, but both can be devastating. Adult rhinoceros beetles can bore into the corm (the underground tuber) of the taro plant. This feeding creates tunnels that ruin the corm for consumption and introduce rot. For a crop so vital to Pacific culture and food security, this damage is a major threat.

Bananas

The beetle attacks banana plants by boring into the pseudostem (the main stalk). This weakens the plant's structure, making it susceptible to toppling over, especially when bearing a heavy bunch of fruit. As documented in recent news reports from Hawaii, this damage can lead to significant crop loss for local farmers who depend on banana production.

Our Mission & Vision

Our Mission

Our mission is to stop the spread of the coconut rhinoceros beetle, use non-toxic, physical, and natural methods for control, and mobilize communities and build infrastructure to support these efforts.

Our Vision

Our vision is to create a future where accessible, non-toxic solutions protect our communities and ecosystems, ensuring that united action today safeguards a prosperous and sustainable tomorrow.
